Executive Summary
StandbyHealth is a physician-supervised digital health platform delivering rapid, affordable medical second opinions. Patients can upload medical records, labs, imaging, and questions. Our AI synthesizes the case, and a licensed US physician reviews, refines, and delivers a structured, patient-friendly written opinion. We combine: 1) Clinical AI for structured summarization and risk triage, 2) Physician oversight for accountability and trust, and 3) Rapid turnaround at affordable pricing. Long term, we aim to build the infrastructure layer for global care navigation, empowering families to make informed medical decisions across borders and healthcare systems.




Problem statements
Possible primary wedge
Adult children living in the US struggle to evaluate and guide medical decisions for aging parents abroad. They often 1) receive fragmented records via WhatsApp PDFs, cannot interpret reports confidently, do not trust local communication fully, and have no accountable medical interpreter/liaison. There is currently no structured, rapid, affordable service that translates international medical care into actionable clarity.


Possible secondary niche entry points
1. ICU/hospitalized family confusion
2. New serious diagnoses (cancer, surgeries, etc.)
3. Conflicting chronic disease management plans




Why now?
1. AI can easily summarize large medical record packets
2. Patients already use AI for medical advice, but it lacks oversight and accountability
3. Cross border care complexity is increasing
4. Rising medical mistrust
5. Physicians get less and less time with patients
6. Other second opinion platforms are slow, costly, and often inaccessible to the masses




Competitors
1. Best doctors - expensive, busy, employer/insurance based, slow turnaround
2. Second opinion services - expensive, slow turnaround
3. AI tools - no accountability or trust
4. Family or friend contact - informal, inconsistent, unscalable, not always available




Solution


Option 1
1. Structured intake for medical history, labs, medications, and questions
2. AI-powered summarization and triage for urgency scoring that is then delivered to physicians
3. Physician review with patient friendly translation (assisted by AI)
4. Final review and written opinion by licensed MD
5. Optional video consults and medication reviews


Option 2
1. Platform where people can upload and store their information
2. AI-chatbot that is 24/7 and every discussion ultimately summarized and reviewed by physician
3. Similar to Lotus Health AI but for additional opinions rather than being the actual primary care




Product
1. Web-based HIPAA compliant platform
2. Patient intake flow
   1. Questionnaire option
   2. Direct uploading of documents
   3. Top concerns
3. MD dashboard: templated case reviews, AI summaries, etc.
4. AI modules
   1. Clinical NLP for summarization
   2. Risk triage engine
   3. Natural language generator for patient summaries
5. “Premium” add ons
   1. 24-48 hour turnaround
   2. Live consult with MD
   3. Care optimization with ability to meet with doctor, pharmacist, other healthcare professionals


Target Market Possibilities
1. Indians who are looking for US doctor time and validation (D2C)
2. Immigrants or adults living in US who have parents or other family in other states/countries (possible focus on India first) (D2C)
3. Anyone who has family member hospitalized acutely (D2C)
4. Multinational companies that are operating in India who want to offer their employees a premium healthcare layer (B2B2C)


Technology Moat
1. Proprietary second opinion dataset powering: 
   1. Fine tuned NLP on real-world cases
   2. Patient-facing summary generation
   3. Feedback loop with MD edits and patients feedback used to refine future AI outputs
   4. Outcome learning loops


Possible risks
1. Physician supply
2. Regulatory aspect of physicians providing educational second opinions that are not considered actual medical care
3. Competition unless there’s a strong unique differentiator
4. AI trust
5. Pricing pressure - lots of free options these days








Gstack ceo review second opinion analysis

 1. STEELMAN: StandbyHealth is building the missing layer between Indian healthcare and the NRI diaspora — a structured, legally defensible second-opinion service where US-licensed physicians review Indian medical records with AI doing the hard preprocessing (OCR on
  WhatsApp-quality scans, non-standard DICOM normalization, bilingual NLP). The moat isn't the AI — it's the trusted delivery system combined with a data flywheel from Indian imaging that no US incumbent will bother to build.
  2. KEY INSIGHT QUOTE: "Dr. Roshan has done informal consults of this type." — This IS the whole business. He's already doing it for free, which means demand is real enough that people ask him personally, he understands the workflow, and the only thing missing is a
  structured delivery layer and a way to charge for it. Everything else is scaffolding around a service that already exists informally.
  3. CHALLENGED PREMISE: Premise 3 — "US physician licensing means legal structure must be physician-supervised." This is underspecified dangerously. US physician licensing is state-by-state. If Dr. Roshan is licensed in Texas, he may not be legally permitted to provide a
   medical opinion to a patient in California, even as a second opinion. The actual safe harbor may be delivering output as "educational review" or "care coordination" rather than a clinical opinion — which has real implications for what the product can say.[a]
